# 01 — Creative Bible

## Premise
The old jump-gates fell silent and the core worlds went dark. Out in the **Voidborne
reach**, authority is whoever can hold a hull and crew it. You are a captain with one
ship and a handful of hands — and the audacity to take what you need by **disabling and
boarding** rather than burning everything to slag.

## Tone
Cold, clean, hopeful-under-pressure. Not grimdark — competent people doing dangerous
work. The win fantasy is *command*, not carnage: a captured frigate flying your colors is
worth more than a debris field.

## Factions (slice)
- **Player** — emerald/green tint. You and anything you capture or man.
- **Hostile** — red tint. Raiders and a heavy line that contest the station.
- **Neutral** — grey tint. The station hub (until captured).
- **Ally** — blue tint. Reserved for hired/captured ships before they re-tint to player.

Faction reads through color tint layered over each class's base hull color, so identity
is instantly legible on screen and on radar.

## Visual language
- **Procedural & blocky-elegant.** Boxes, capsules, cylinders, a torus station — readable
  silhouettes that scale with class. Bigger = slower, deadlier, harder to take.
- **Emissive accents.** Engine glow scales with throttle; weapons and explosions are
  unshaded emissives that pop against a dark nebula sky.
- **The deck is intimate.** A lit interior with consoles and humanoid crew — a deliberate
  contrast to the cold exterior, reinforcing "these are *your people*."

## Audio language
Synth-procedural. Lasers bite, beams hum low, explosions are filtered noise, captures
resolve on a rising major interval — a small reward signature for taking a hull intact.

## Naming
Ships get terse call-signs (`Raider-3`, `Ironclaw`, `Dread Maw`, `Halcyon`). Crew get
`Name-NN` tags (`Vega-42`) — placeholder-personal, enough to make "order *that one* to
follow" feel specific.

## North star scene
Your battered corvette holds station-side as a disabled hostile frigate drifts under your
guns. Marines breach; the boarding bar fills; the hull flips to green and falls into
formation behind you — while on the deck, the crew you just freed up walks to your side.
